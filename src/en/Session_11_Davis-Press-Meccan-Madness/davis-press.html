Davis Press - Meccan Madness

A case study on the publication decision of a controversial book

Overview

This case study examines the decision-making process of Davis Press regarding the publication of the controversial book "Meccan Madness". The case highlights the tensions between editorial freedom, ethical responsibility, and economic interests in a highly sensitive religious and geopolitical context.

1. Background on Davis Press

Davis Press is a medium-sized publishing house with a long tradition in publishing academic and literary works. The publisher has built a reputation for quality and integrity and is known for its careful selection of manuscripts. Carol Davis, the publishing director, now faces one of the most difficult decisions of her career.

Founded

1952

Employees

85

Annual Publications

120

2. The Book "Meccan Madness"

"Meccan Madness" is a work by author Jonathan Taajwar, who describes himself as a "lapsed Muslim". The book contains highly controversial depictions of Islamic religious practices and institutions. The plot revolves around a Muslim woman who attempts to take control of the Kaaba in Mecca - Islam's holiest shrine.

Key Aspects of the Book:

Aspect Description
Literary Value High stylistic quality, innovative narrative structure
Controversial Content Direct provocation of Islamic values through the fictional occupation of the Kaaba by a woman
Market Potential High interest due to controversy, potential bestseller similar to Rushdie's "The Satanic Verses"
Risks Possible protests, boycotts, violent acts, and even death threats against the publisher, author, and staff

3. Scandalous Dimensions of the Book

The explosive nature of the book "Meccan Madness" extends across several dimensions:

Critical Aspects:

  • Religious Provocation: The plot represents a direct provocation for the Islamic world. The fictional occupation of the Kaaba by a woman and a revolutionary movement contradicts fundamental religious and cultural principles of Islam and could be interpreted as blasphemous.
  • Parallels to Salman Rushdie: The striking parallels to the case of Salman Rushdie and his work "The Satanic Verses" are unmistakable. Rushdie's novel triggered a global scandal that led to death threats, assassination attempts, and a fatwa. Davis Press risks igniting a similar storm of indignation.
  • Geopolitical Context: The tense geopolitical situation after 9/11, the Iraq War, and the Guantanamo incidents has intensified anti-Western resentment in many Muslim countries. In this climate, publication could provoke particularly explosive reactions.
  • Security Risks: The personal safety of all involved - especially the publisher's staff and the author - is at stake. Carol Davis bears the moral responsibility for protecting her employees.

4. Ethical Dilemmas

The decision about publishing "Meccan Madness" presents Davis Press with several ethical dilemmas:

Central Questions:

  • Freedom of Expression vs. Respect: How far does editorial freedom extend when religious feelings could be hurt? Is the deliberate provocation of religious values through literature justified?
  • Economic Interests vs. Ethics: Should potential commercial success outweigh ethical concerns? Is it acceptable to profit from a potential crisis?
  • Literary Value vs. Social Impact: Does the literary quality justify the possible negative social consequences and security risks?
  • Publisher's Responsibility: What responsibility does a publisher bear for the content it publishes and for the safety of its employees?

5. Stakeholder Analysis

The decision affects various stakeholders with different perspectives:

Stakeholder Interests Possible Position
Carol Davis / Publishing Management Financial success, publisher's reputation, staff safety Divided between economic opportunities, freedom of expression, and security concerns
Jonathan Taajwar (Author) Publication of his work, artistic freedom, personal safety For publication, possibly aware of the risks
Islamic Communities Respect for religious beliefs and sanctuaries Strong resistance to publication, possible protests
Readership Access to literary valuable and controversial works Varied, depending on personal values and interest in controversies
Publisher's Employees Job security, personal safety, ethical principles Concerned about security risks, possibly divided regarding publication

6. Options for Action

Davis Press faces various options for action, each with different potential consequences:

Option 1: Complete Publication

  • Advantages: Preserving artistic integrity, potential commercial success, defending freedom of expression
  • Disadvantages: Significant security risks for the publisher and staff, possible violent acts, international tensions

Option 2: Publication with Changes

  • Advantages: Reducing controversy, maintaining literary value, mitigating security risks
  • Disadvantages: Compromising artistic vision, possible resistance from the author, accusations of self-censorship

Option 3: Rejection of Publication

  • Advantages: Avoiding controversies and security risks, protecting publisher's reputation and staff
  • Disadvantages: Lost economic opportunities, possible criticism for self-censorship, restriction of artistic freedom

Option 4: Delayed Publication with Contextualization

  • Advantages: Enables dialogue and preparation, shows responsibility, security measures can be taken
  • Disadvantages: Delay of potential success, could intensify controversy, security risks remain

7. Discussion Questions

For group work: Discuss the following questions in your group and prepare a brief presentation of your conclusions.

  1. Which option should Carol Davis choose and why?
  2. Which ethical principles should take precedence in this decision?
  3. How does this case compare to Salman Rushdie's "The Satanic Verses," and what lessons can be drawn from it?
  4. How could Davis Press communicate with the various stakeholders to explain the chosen decision?
  5. What security measures should the publisher take if publication is decided?
  6. Is it ethically justifiable to profit from a deliberate provocation?

8. Relevance for Practice

This case study illustrates challenges that can arise in various industries:

  • Media and Publishing: Balancing freedom of expression and social responsibility in a globalized world
  • Business Ethics: Balance between economic interests and ethical principles when they conflict
  • Crisis Management: Preparation for and handling of potentially dangerous controversial situations
  • Stakeholder Management: Considering various interests in decision-making processes with far-reaching consequences
  • Intercultural Sensitivity: Understanding religious and cultural values in a globalized business world

The insights from this case can be applied to many situations where companies must make ethically complex decisions that touch on cultural and religious sensitivities.

Conclusion

The "Davis Press - Meccan Madness" case illustrates the complex trade-offs between artistic freedom, economic interests, and social responsibility in a highly sensitive religious and geopolitical context. It shows that business decisions often have to be made in a field of tension between different values and interests.

As one commentator aptly noted: "Can a bestseller be justified if the price is a potential fatwa against the publisher and author?" This question highlights the core dilemma of the case. Carol Davis must decide whether she wants to go down in history as a brave defender of freedom of expression or whether the publication is a risky marketing maneuver with potentially dangerous consequences.

The analysis of this case provides valuable insights into ethical decision-making processes and the importance of careful consideration of all relevant factors. Regardless of the decision ultimately made, a transparent and reflective decision-making process is crucial for a company's integrity - especially when the consequences can be so far-reaching.
